## Where I fit in a group
I tend to settle into a group as the person who brings structure, clarity, and forward momentum. I’m usually the one who sees the system end‑to‑end, identifies where ambiguity will cause problems later, and then builds the patterns, documentation, and architectural guardrails that keep everyone aligned. I’m comfortable being hands‑on when the team is blocked, but I’m just as comfortable stepping back and shaping the bigger picture so the team can move faster with fewer surprises.

I naturally take ownership of the technical foundation — the architecture, the infrastructure patterns, the DevOps workflows, the naming conventions, the onboarding clarity — because I know those decisions compound over time. I’m the person who will dig into a problem until I understand the root cause, write down the reasoning behind the solution, and make sure the team has a repeatable path going forward.

At the same time, I’m collaborative. I like working with people who bring different perspectives, and I’m good at translating between product, engineering, and operations so everyone understands the “why” behind a decision. I don’t need to be the loudest voice in the room; I just want the system to make sense, the team to be aligned, and the work to be sustainable.
